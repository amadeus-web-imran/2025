**Page 2: The Blueprint**

The architecture of **Dawn** is deliberately simple, decentralized, and resilient, serving as a direct counterpoint to the centralized, data-hungry models of the current web.

**The Technical Foundation: Federated Repositories**

At its core, **Dawn** is built on the simplicity of **plain text files** and **Git**. Rather than relying on a single, massive repository, the network is composed of a federation of smaller, independent Git repositories hosted on various websites. Each repository acts as an autonomous hub for a specific group, be it a family, a company, a religious organization, or an educational institution.

This federated model provides a unique blend of autonomy and connectivity. Each group maintains full control over its own content and ethical standards, while remaining part of a larger interconnected ecosystem. The entire network is designed to be easily turned into websites, with a clear hierarchical structure of **networks, websites, subdomains, and sections** that are navigable via breadcrumbs and menus. The entire system is lightweight enough to run on a personal server, a mobile device, or a tablet, and the use of Markdown and tools like Obsidian makes content creation accessible to everyone.

**The Social and Ethical Framework**

**Dawn** is governed not by algorithms or a centralized authority, but by a social contract of **trust and transparency**. Every group and individual is expected to declare their values in public text files, providing a clear ethical blueprint from the start. Authority is based on expertise and the public record of contributions visible in a repository’s Git history.

This system has no need for enforcement. When a fundamental disagreement arises, the solution is not conflict, but a graceful separation. The affected individual or group is free to create their own personal space or start a new network, a philosophy that prioritizes growth through new creation rather than an endless cycle of divisive debate.
